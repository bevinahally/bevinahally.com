My favorite MOOCs



Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) have democratized education and has made quality education accessible. I do not view MOOCs as a replacement for the traditional collegiate experience, but as a great way to gain exposure to new fields of study or to gain specific skills in a structured environment.

What's the catch? I've seen a whole bunch of snake oil salesemen sell mediocre courses at a hefty price, much to my chagrin. Compared to a few years ago, finding good courses is like finding a needle in a haystack.

I was lucky since I barely spent any money on these courses thanks to discounts or my employer's generosity. The sticker price may or may not be worth it to you. Additionally, for the employers reading this post, I think that it is great RoI for a company to purchase these courses as a part of new employee training.

I've put together a list of courses that I found particularly useful in my development and my experiences.

Course Name Provider Comments
Machine Learning by Andrew Ng Coursera The quintessential MOOC. Prof. Ng is a great teacher and an inspiration. There really isn't much more to say than how influential this man is for popularizing machine learning.
CS 101 by David Evans Udacity I found CS 101 a great course to get my feet wet with the fundamentals of CS and to start thinking about problems algorithmically.
Python for Data Analysis by Jose Portilla Udemy Coming from a familiarity with R, I had to get up to speed in Python fairly quickly for a job and I found Portilla's course very useful in getting familiarized with the basics of Python.
SQL for Newbs: Beginner Data Analysis Udemy Are you interested in learning SQL but have no idea where to start? If so, this course by David Kim and Peter Sefton is a fun way to learn the basics of SQL.
Taming Big Data with Apache Spark and Python Udemy Ever wanted to explore the wonderful technology called Spark? In this small and simple course, Frank Kane introduces you to the basics
